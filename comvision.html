Introduction

As an enthusiast of indoor gardening, I wanted to track the growth of my potted plants over time to study their health and development. This project aims to measure the growth of plants using a series of time-lapse images.

Method

The series of images were collected daily at noon to ensure consistent lighting. We utilized YOLO to detect plants in each image and then measured the bounding box dimensions to estimate plant size. These measurements were then plotted to observe growth over time.

Analysis of Results

The plotted data reveals that the plant showed a consistent growth pattern, with an unexpected surge in size during the third week. This anomaly sparked a further investigation into the plant's care routine during that period.

Analysis of Vision Algorithms

The YOLO algorithm successfully identified potted plants in 95% of the images. We encountered challenges with low contrast images, which required pre-processing for better plant detection. Adjustments were made to the model's parameters to improve accuracy in subsequent tests.
